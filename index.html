Who we are

A friendly plot in the valley

Down at Roscoe Plantation we are an inclusive, thriving allotment society. We are a group of tenants with all different skills, knowledge and ability who share the same aim: building a community in the valley to promote gardening.

Our membership is diverse and intergenerational, and we look after the plot together. Whether you have grown vegetables for years or have never picked up a trowel, there is a place for you here, and plenty of people happy to share what they know.

Membership

Join the society

Membership is open to anyone with a plot at Roscoe Plantation. For a small yearly fee you become part of the society and share in everything we have built together.

National membership

Membership of the National Allotment Society, with its advice and support behind you.

Community hut

A shared hut for members to use, and to hire for your own gatherings.

Tools & polytunnel

Shared community tools and a polytunnel that all members can use.

Woodchip & manure

Free woodchip and manure for members, to feed and mulch your plot.

Hour of power

A helping hand to get new tenants started and on top of a fresh plot.

Insured & social

Public liability insurance on every plot, plus monthly meetings and social events.

£5 / year per plot

That is the whole membership fee. It covers your place in the society and everything listed above.

What's on

Our year in the valley

The society meets through the year for growing, sharing and the odd party. Here is the shape of a typical season; exact dates for the year ahead are shared with members.

Early spring
AGM & seed sowing — the year's plans, and getting the first seeds in.
May
Plant sales — our well-loved early and late May sales, with home-grown plants galore.
Summer
Society BBQ — a get-together on the plots when everything is in full swing.
September
Fuddle & competition — a shared spread and our friendly allotment competition.
Autumn / winter
Bonfire night & Christmas party — rounding off the year together.
Monthly
Members' meetings — news, plans and a chance to get involved.

Members get the full calendar with confirmed dates. Get in touch to be added to the list.

Plant sale

The May plant sales

Each May the society holds its plant sales: a great spread of home-grown flowers, herbs and vegetables, raised by members and sold to keep the plot thriving. It is one of the highlights of our year, and everyone is welcome.

From achillea and cosmos to chillies, courgettes and romanesco, our growers raise well over a hundred and fifty varieties between them. Come along, pick up something for your garden, and have a chat with the people who grew it.

Our plot

An accessible, welcoming space

We want Roscoe Plantation to be open to everyone. Working with our members, we are improving the plot so that it stays accessible, secure and welcoming, whatever your experience or means.

That means steady paths and ramps in place of awkward steps, a secure entrance for our shared resources, an expanded tool library, a better community polytunnel and raised beds for easier planting. The aim is simple: a place where anyone can grow, learn from each other and feel they belong.
